By Education level


We can see, the mean employment rate isn't changed too much in the past 30 years, it's quite stable.

However, In terms of median rate, which can show the real things. we can see, only the category 'below secondary' has huge fluctuation, it dropped 40% between 2016 to 2018, but the rest two categories didn't change much during that time.

In 2009, there is a big rise in the category 'below secondary', I think it's because the financial crisis happened in 2008, but surprisedly, the `above secondary` categories doesn't drop.

Global Unemployment

2020 and 2021 are tough years due to the pandemic, the unemployment rate increased a lot.
By changing the slider, we can see the colour on the map getting darker in 2020 compared to 2019. But better in 2022

The graph on the left has two horizontal lines, the orange one is the mean unemployment rate and the red line is the 75% of the data.

There are two important years in this graph, 2008 and 2019. the global economy is getting better in the 21st century, so the unemployment rate drooped, but the financial crisis happened in 2008 and this is why the unemployment rate backed to 2004.

As mentioned above, the Covid pandemic also caused people to lose their jobs, but when we are used to it, when we have the vaccine, the global economy will be back to normal.
